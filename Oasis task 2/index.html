A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India


The man who loved innovation (1931-2015)


Here's a time-line of Kalam's life:


  • 1951 - Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1960 - After completing his schooling, Kalam graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, with a degree in physics in 1954. He then moved to Madras (now Chennai) to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969 - Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist. He later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a significant role in India's space program.
  • 1980 - Kalam was appointed as the project director for India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle program, which successfully launched the Rohini satellite into space in 1980.
  • 1992 - He became the Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1998 - Dr. Kalam served as the Chief Project Coordinator for Pokhran-II nuclear tests, which made India a nuclear weapons state.
  • 2002 - Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007. He was known as the "People's President" for his humble and approachable nature.
  • 2015 - Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, after suffering a cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
